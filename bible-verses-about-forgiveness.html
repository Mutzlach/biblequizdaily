What the Bible Really Says About Forgiveness

Forgiveness is at the heart of the gospel — both receiving it from God and extending it to others.

God's forgiveness is limitless and eager.

The 21 verses below are drawn from across both Testaments, each with a short commentary explaining what it means in its original context and how it applies today. Every verse is from the World English Bible (Public Domain).

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Bible say about forgiveness? ▼
1 John 1:9 is the foundational promise: If we confess our sins, he is faithful and righteous to forgive us the sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness. Matthew 6:14-15 connects our forgiveness with forgiving others.
Does the Bible say I must forgive others? ▼
Yes - Matthew 6:14-15 is among the clearest passages. Forgiveness is not optional in the biblical framework - it flows from receiving God's forgiveness. Colossians 3:13 commands us to forgive as the Lord forgave us.
What is the difference between forgiveness and reconciliation? ▼
Forgiveness is a decision made by the offended party - it releases the debt and the bitterness. Reconciliation is the restoration of a relationship, which requires both parties. You can forgive without restoring a harmful relationship.
