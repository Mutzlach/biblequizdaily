What the Bible Really Says About Pride & Humility

Humility is the posture God blesses — pride is the root of almost every sin.

The kingdom operates on an upside-down economy.

The 21 verses below are drawn from across both Testaments, each with a short commentary explaining what it means in its original context and how it applies today. Every verse is from the World English Bible (Public Domain).

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Bible say about pride? ▼
Proverbs 16:18 gives the famous warning: Pride goes before destruction, and a haughty spirit before a fall. James 4:6 adds: God resists the proud, but gives grace to the humble.
What does the Bible say about humility? ▼
Philippians 2:3-8 is the key passage, presenting Christ as the supreme example: Do nothing out of selfish ambition or vain conceit, but in humility consider others better than yourselves.
How can I become more humble according to the Bible? ▼
James 4:10 gives the direct instruction: Humble yourselves in the sight of the Lord, and he will exalt you. Humility is cultivated by prayer, service, honest self-examination, and deliberately choosing others' interests over our own.
