About This Bible Numbers Quiz

Ask any regular Bible reader how many plagues God sent on Egypt and most will say ten. Ask them how old Methuselah was when he died and many will say "very old." Ask them how many days Jesus fasted in the wilderness and some will say forty. Ask them how many wise men visited Jesus and watch the confidence disappear.

Numbers in Scripture are specific and intentional. The Bible records exact ages, durations, counts, and measurements for a reason. This quiz tests whether you've been reading carefully enough to remember them.

From the 603,550 men counted in Israel's first census to the 144,000 sealed in Revelation, from Methuselah's 969 years to Solomon's 700 wives, from 40 days in the wilderness to 40 years wandering — the numbers of Scripture tell a story of their own.

Why Biblical Numbers Matter

Numbers in the ancient world carried symbolic weight that modern readers often miss. Seven represented completeness and rest — God rested on the seventh day, Naaman dipped seven times, the walls of Jericho were circled seven days. Forty represented testing and transition — forty days of flood, forty years in the wilderness, forty days of Jesus's temptation.

Knowing these numbers helps you see patterns across Scripture. When you notice that Moses fasted forty days on the mountain and Jesus fasted forty days in the desert, you start to see how the New Testament echoes the Old. When you realise that Solomon's 700 wives and 300 concubines directly followed God's warning against multiplying wives, the tragedy of his later years makes complete sense.

The specific numbers also guard against vague, generalised reading of Scripture. The Bible doesn't say Noah was old when the flood came — it says he was 600 years old. It doesn't say the rain lasted a long time — it says forty days. Precision matters because it points to the reliability and intentionality of the text.

How to Prepare for This Numbers Quiz

Focus on the number 40: It appears more than almost any other number in significant contexts. Moses, Elijah, Jesus, and the Israelites all experienced forty-day or forty-year periods. Know what happened in each.

Learn the ages of key patriarchs: Methuselah (969), Adam (930), Noah (950), and Abraham (175) are commonly tested. The contrast between pre-flood lifespans and post-flood lifespans is a significant biblical detail.

Count the tribes, disciples, and judges: Twelve tribes, twelve apostles, twelve minor prophets. These aligned numbers appear repeatedly in Scripture and in quiz questions.

Know Solomon's excesses: 700 wives, 300 concubines, 40,000 stalls of horses, 12,000 horsemen — Solomon's numbers are frequently tested because they dramatically illustrate his departure from God's instructions for kings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these questions about Bible numerology or literal numbers? These questions test your knowledge of specific numbers as they actually appear in the KJV Bible text — ages, quantities, time periods, and counts. This is biblical numerics, not numerology. We're asking what the Bible says, not assigning mystical meanings.

How specific do the answers need to be? Very specific. The quiz gives you four possible answers and you select the correct one. In some cases the differences are small — 40 days vs 50 days, for example — so careful reading of the question is essential.

What's the hardest category of numbers to remember? Based on Scripture study patterns, ages of patriarchs and exact time periods tend to be the hardest. Most people know approximate numbers but struggle with precision.

Are these suitable for Sunday School? Yes, for older students and adults. The hard difficulty means this works best for Bible study groups or individuals with solid Bible knowledge rather than younger children.
