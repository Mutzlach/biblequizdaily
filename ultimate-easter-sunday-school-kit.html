Why Your Easter Activities Should Teach the Full Story

Here's something worth saying plainly: Easter Sunday is not a standalone event. The Resurrection only means what it means because of everything that came before it — the Triumphal Entry, Gethsemane, the betrayal, the trial, the Cross, the burial. Children who only ever hear "Jesus is risen!" without understanding why He died and what the empty tomb proved are missing the foundation of the gospel.

The best Easter activities don't just celebrate — they teach. They walk children and families through the story from beginning to end, so that by the time Easter morning arrives, the declaration of Matthew 28:6 lands with the full weight of everything that preceded it.

"He is not here: for he is risen, as he said. Come, see the place where the Lord lay."

— Matthew 28:6 (KJV)

Activity-based learning — trivia, games, treasure hunts — is one of the most powerful tools we have for this. Children remember what they do far longer than what they hear. When a child physically searches for a clue about Gethsemane, answers a trivia question about the Last Supper, or marks a BINGO square for "Barabbas," those moments stick. The story becomes their story.

Every activity in the Complete Easter Kit was designed with this principle in mind. From the first trivia question to the treasure hunt's final clue, children are walking through the Easter story from Palm Sunday to the Ascension — actively, joyfully, and in the Word of God.

"Train up a child in the way he should go: and when he is old, he will not depart from it."

— Proverbs 22:6 (KJV)

Christ-centered Easter activities give children a story to hold onto — not just a holiday to celebrate.
