What Jesus' Miracles Teach Us

When Jesus healed the blind man in John 9, He wasn't just restoring physical sight. He was showing that He's the light of the world‚Äîthe one who opens spiritual eyes to see God. When He raised Lazarus from the dead in John 11, He was demonstrating that He has power over death itself‚Äîa preview of His own resurrection.

The miracles authenticated Jesus' claims. When John the Baptist sent disciples to ask if Jesus was the Messiah, Jesus pointed to His miracles as proof: the blind see, the lame walk, lepers are cleansed, the deaf hear, the dead are raised. These weren't random acts of kindness‚Äîthey were the very signs Isaiah prophesied the Messiah would perform.

But the miracles also revealed Jesus' compassion. He didn't heal people to draw crowds or prove a point. When He saw the widow of Nain following her son's funeral procession, He had compassion on her and raised the boy. When people brought Him the sick and demon-possessed, He healed them all. Every miracle met a real need for a real person who was suffering.

And here's what's remarkable: Jesus gave His disciples the same power. After His resurrection, He told them they'd do even greater works than He did‚Äînot because they were stronger, but because the Holy Spirit would work through them. The miracles in Acts prove Jesus kept that promise. The same power that raised Jesus from the dead is available to believers today.

The Most Important Miracles to Know

The Gospels record dozens of miracles. Here are the ones that appear most often in this jesus miracles quiz questions:

Feeding the 5,000 (All four Gospels): The only miracle recorded in all four Gospels besides the resurrection. Jesus took five loaves and two fish, blessed them, and fed over 5,000 men plus women and children. Twelve baskets of leftovers remained. This showed Jesus as the bread of life who satisfies spiritual hunger.

Walking on water (Matthew 14, Mark 6, John 6): After feeding the 5,000, Jesus sent the disciples across the Sea of Galilee while He prayed. In the middle of the night, He walked to them on the water. Peter stepped out to meet Him but started sinking when he took his eyes off Jesus.

Calming the storm (Matthew 8, Mark 4, Luke 8): A fierce storm threatened to swamp the boat while Jesus slept. The terrified disciples woke Him. He stood up, rebuked the wind and waves, and everything became perfectly calm. Then He asked why they had so little faith.

Raising Lazarus (John 11): Jesus' friend Lazarus died and was buried for four days. When Jesus arrived, Martha and Mary were grieving. Jesus wept with them, then called Lazarus out of the tomb. This miracle led directly to the religious leaders plotting Jesus' death‚ÄîHis power was too threatening.

Healing the blind man (John 9): Jesus healed a man born blind by putting mud on his eyes and telling him to wash. The Pharisees interrogated everyone involved because Jesus did this on the Sabbath. The healed man's testimony is powerful: "One thing I know‚ÄîI was blind but now I see!"

Water into wine (John 2): Jesus' first miracle, performed at a wedding in Cana when the wine ran out. He turned six stone water jars‚Äîeach holding 20-30 gallons‚Äîinto the best wine anyone had tasted. This revealed His glory and His disciples believed in Him.

Common Questions About This Miracles Quiz

Which Gospels should I read? All four Gospels record miracles, but John's Gospel focuses most on their meaning. If you're short on time, read Matthew 8-9 (multiple miracles), Mark 4-5 (storm, demons, healing), and John 9-11 (blind man, Lazarus). That will cover many of the questions in this quiz.

Are all of Jesus' miracles included? No‚ÄîJesus performed countless miracles that weren't recorded individually. John says if everything Jesus did were written down, the world couldn't contain the books. This quiz focuses on the specific miracles described in detail in the Gospels.

Will this help my faith? Reading about Jesus' miracles reminds you that nothing is impossible for God. The same Jesus who calmed storms and raised the dead is alive today. While He may not work the same way now as He did then, His power hasn't diminished. These stories build faith in who Jesus is.

Can I use this for Sunday School? Definitely! Many teachers use this miracles of jesus quiz after teaching about Jesus' ministry. It works great for upper elementary through adult classes. The instant feedback makes it perfect for group discussions about what each miracle reveals about Jesus.
