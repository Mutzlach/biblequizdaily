What the Bible Really Says About Hope

Biblical hope is not a wish — it is a confident expectation grounded in God's faithfulness and His resurrection power.

The 21 verses below are drawn from across both Testaments, each with a short commentary explaining what it means in its original context and how it applies today. Every verse is from the World English Bible (Public Domain).

Jeremiah 29:11

For I know the thoughts that I think toward you, says Yahweh, thoughts of peace, and not of evil, to give you hope and a future.

God's famous promise — spoken to exiles in Babylon, not prosperity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Bible say about hope? ▼
Romans 15:13 names God as the source: Now may the God of hope fill you with all joy and peace in believing. Jeremiah 29:11 gives His promise: I know the thoughts that I think toward you, to give you hope and a future.
What is the biblical definition of hope? ▼
Biblical hope is not wishful thinking - it is confident expectation based on God's character and promises. Hebrews 11:1 links it directly to faith: faith is assurance of things hoped for. The Christian's ultimate hope is the resurrection of Christ.
What Bible verse gives hope in difficult times? ▼
Lamentations 3:22-23 is particularly powerful because it was written in the middle of catastrophe: It is because of Yahweh's loving kindness that we are not consumed. They are new every morning. Also Romans 8:28 and Psalm 46:1-2.
