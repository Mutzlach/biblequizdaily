Why the Easter Story Deserves More Than an Egg Hunt

Here's something most Sunday School teachers feel but rarely say out loud: Easter Sunday can feel like we're fighting against the culture for our students' attention. Chocolate eggs, Spring Break, Easter baskets — all of it is fun, but none of it points to the empty tomb.

The Resurrection is the single most important event in all of human history. Paul says it plainly in 1 Corinthians 15:17 — "And if Christ be not raised, your faith is vain; ye are yet in your sins." Everything we believe hinges on that morning. Our children need to know this story deeply — not just that Jesus rose, but why He was arrested, what happened at Gethsemane, who Pilate was, what the veil tearing meant, and what the Great Commission calls us to do.

"Go ye therefore, and teach all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost."

— Matthew 28:19 (KJV)

Trivia questions are a surprisingly powerful teaching tool because they reveal gaps. When a child answers a question wrong, they become genuinely curious about the right answer. That curiosity is a door — and Scripture walks right through it.

Activity-based learning also helps children retain truth far longer than passive listening. When your class has worked through 120 questions covering every stage of Holy Week, they aren't just entertained — they are equipped.
