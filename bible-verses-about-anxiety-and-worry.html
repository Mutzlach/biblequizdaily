What the Bible Really Says About Anxiety & Worry

When worry overwhelms, Scripture points us back to God's peace and provision.

The Bible addresses anxiety more than almost any other emotion.

The 21 verses below are drawn from across both Testaments, each with a short commentary explaining what it means in its original context and how it applies today. Every verse is from the World English Bible (Public Domain).

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Bible say about anxiety? ▼
The Bible addresses anxiety directly in Philippians 4:6-7, instructing us to bring everything to God in prayer with thanksgiving, and promising the peace of God will guard our hearts. Other key passages include 1 Peter 5:7, Matthew 6:25-34, and Isaiah 41:10.
What Bible verse is best for anxiety? ▼
Philippians 4:6-7 is most cited. Paul wrote it from prison: In nothing be anxious, but in everything, by prayer and petition with thanksgiving, let your requests be made known to God. Others frequently turned to include 1 Peter 5:7, Isaiah 41:10, and Matthew 11:28-29.
Is anxiety a sin according to the Bible? ▼
The Bible does not classify anxiety as a sin the way pride or dishonesty are. Jesus addresses anxiety with compassion in Matthew 6:25-34. The command do not be anxious is better understood as an invitation to bring worry to God rather than a condemnation of the feeling itself.
