Why the Old Testament Deserves Serious Study

There is a tendency in modern ministry to treat the Old Testament as background — the part of the Bible you reference occasionally before getting to the New Testament. This is a mistake that leaves students with a dangerously incomplete understanding of Scripture.

The New Testament cannot be properly understood without the Old. The sacrificial system foreshadows the Cross. The Passover lamb points directly to Christ. The covenants with Abraham, Moses, and David trace an unbroken line to the Gospel. When Jesus says "I am the resurrection and the life," it lands with full weight only for students who have stood at Lazarus's tomb — but also for those who have seen God breathe life into Adam, who know that the same God who created life in Genesis 1 can restore it.

Quiz-based learning is one of the most effective tools for Old Testament education because the format forces students to engage with specifics — not general themes, but actual names, places, events, and verses. A student who can answer "What were the names of Joseph's two sons born in Egypt?" (Manasseh and Ephraim — Genesis 41:51-52) has genuinely absorbed that passage in a way that passive listening rarely achieves.

The Old Testament Bible Quiz Pack is built on this principle. Every question is designed to test real comprehension of the text, grounded in the KJV, with the verse reference provided so that every wrong answer becomes a teaching moment.
