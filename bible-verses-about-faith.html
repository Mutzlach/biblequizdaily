What the Bible Really Says About Faith

Faith is the foundation of the Christian life — trust in who God is, not just what He does.

It is the evidence of things unseen and the substance of things hoped for.

The 21 verses below are drawn from across both Testaments, each with a short commentary explaining what it means in its original context and how it applies today. Every verse is from the World English Bible (Public Domain).

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Bible say about faith? ▼
Hebrews 11:1 defines faith: Now faith is assurance of things hoped for, proof of things not seen. Romans 10:17 explains its source: Faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God.
How do you increase faith according to the Bible? ▼
Romans 10:17 is the key: Faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God. Regular engagement with Scripture grows faith. Mark 9:24 shows that admitting weak faith and bringing it to God is itself an act of faith.
What is the difference between faith and belief? ▼
In the New Testament, the Greek word pistis covers both faith and belief. However, James 2:19 distinguishes the merely intellectual from the genuinely transforming: The demons also believe, and shudder. Biblical faith produces action and life change.
