Why the Women of the Bible Deserve More Than a Passing Mention

Scripture is full of women who acted with greater faith than the men around them. Rahab hid the spies when Israel's own men were afraid. Deborah led Israel to victory when Barak refused to go without her. Esther risked her life to save her people when the men of the court kept silent. Mary Magdalene was the first to the tomb and the first to see the risen Christ — when the disciples had scattered.

Yet women's Bible study resources often treat these figures as illustrations rather than as people with stories worth knowing in detail. This quiz is designed to change that. It asks specific questions about these women's lives, words, and actions — because the details are where the theology lives.

Ruth's declaration to Naomi — 'Whither thou goest, I will go' — is more than a famous verse. It is the decision of a Gentile woman to identify herself with the God of Israel, and it puts her in the direct lineage of Jesus Christ. Esther's 'if I perish, I perish' is not just courage. It is the posture of someone who has submitted their life to a larger purpose. These women deserve to be known.
