About This Armor of God Bible Quiz

The Armor of God passage in Ephesians 6:10-18 is among the most practical and beloved instructions in all of Paul's letters. Written while Paul was himself a prisoner in Rome—likely chained to a Roman soldier—he uses military imagery to teach believers how to stand against spiritual attack. This quiz explores every detail of that passage, from the command to "be strong in the Lord and in the power of his might" to the injunction to pray "always with all prayer and supplication in the Spirit."

Questions cover the nature of the enemy we face—not flesh and blood, but "principalities, against powers, against the rulers of the darkness of this world, against spiritual wickedness in high places" (Ephesians 6:12). You'll be tested on each piece of armor: what it represents theologically, how it functions defensively, which piece is the only offensive weapon, and why Paul emphasizes putting on the "whole" armor rather than partial protection.

Whether you're a new believer learning about spiritual warfare for the first time or a seasoned student of Paul's letters, this quiz will challenge and strengthen your understanding. Each question uses the King James Version, so you're learning Scripture exactly as written in one of history's most trusted translations.

Why the Armor of God Matters for Every Believer

Paul's armor passage addresses a reality that many Christians overlook: the Christian life is a warfare, not a vacation. "For we wrestle not against flesh and blood" (Ephesians 6:12) corrects the common mistake of treating spiritual problems as if they were merely social or psychological. Behind the visible conflicts in our lives—temptation, discouragement, doubt, division in the church—lies an invisible spiritual dimension requiring spiritual weapons.

The armor Paul describes is not something we manufacture through effort or discipline. Each piece flows directly from who God is and what Christ has done. The belt of truth is God's truth revealed in Scripture. The breastplate of righteousness is Christ's righteousness imputed to believers. The shoes represent the peace Christ secured through the cross. The shield is faith in God's promises. The helmet is the assurance of salvation already won by Christ. The sword is God's own Word given to us.

Understanding the armor also transforms how we approach daily life. When doubt assaults your mind, you lift the shield of faith—not by manufacturing more willpower, but by believing God's specific promises in His Word. When temptation attacks, you wield the sword of the Spirit as Jesus did in the wilderness, declaring "it is written." Studying the armor doesn't just help you pass a quiz; it equips you for every challenge of the Christian life.

Study Tips for the Armor of God Quiz

Memorize Ephesians 6:10-18 as a whole passage: Reading the armor in its full context reveals how each piece relates to the others. Notice the repeated command to "stand" (verses 11, 13, 14)—our goal is not conquest but holding ground Christ has already won.

Learn each piece in order with its spiritual meaning: Belt of truth, breastplate of righteousness, shoes of peace, shield of faith, helmet of salvation, sword of the Spirit. Each piece corresponds to a specific area of spiritual vulnerability. Know all six in order and what each one represents.

Identify the only offensive weapon: Five of the six pieces are defensive armor. The sword of the Spirit—the Word of God—is the only weapon for going on the offensive. Jesus modeled this in Matthew 4 when He responded to every temptation with "It is written."

Study the Roman soldier parallel: Paul's audience would have pictured a fully equipped Roman legionary. The belt held everything together, the breastplate protected vital organs, the hobnailed sandals gave traction, the large shield could block burning arrows, the helmet protected from head blows, and the short sword was used in close combat.
