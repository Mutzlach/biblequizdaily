60 Bible Trivia Questions for Women's Ministry Night (KJV)

📅 April 2026 ⏱ 10 min read 📖 All KJV 🆓 Free to use
⬇ Jump to the Questions

A women's ministry group enjoying a Bible trivia night using the Bible Trivia Experience kit.

Picture this: your ladies are seated around a table, Bibles open, pens ready, and already laughing before you've asked the first question. Someone confidently announces the wrong answer. Half the room groans. The other half erupts. And then — without you engineering it — someone asks, "Wait, so what really happened to Rahab?" And the conversation begins.

That is what a well-run Bible trivia night does. It creates an entry point into God's Word that feels joyful rather than intimidating, competitive rather than coercive. It works equally well for your longtime Bible students and for the woman who quietly showed up for the first time wondering if she belongs.

These 60 KJV-based questions are organised into four rounds, each built for a different dimension of the women's ministry experience. Use all four for a full evening, or pick one round for a quick icebreaker before Bible study. Every answer includes the Scripture reference so your group can go straight to the text.

How to Use These Questions at Your Women's Event

These questions are flexible enough for almost any format. Here are three approaches that work well for women's ministry groups:

🏆
Team Competition
Divide into teams of 3–5. One round per team, read questions aloud, tally scores. Best for groups of 12 or more where friendly rivalry adds energy.
⚡
Speed Round
Each woman answers individually on paper. First one to finish a full round correctly wins. Works for smaller groups who enjoy the thrill of a time limit.
💬
Table Talk Icebreaker
Use 5–10 questions at the start of a Bible study to warm up the room. After each answer, add one discussion prompt. Zero pressure, all connection.

Tip for mixed knowledge levels: Start with Round 4 (Icebreaker questions) — the most accessible round — before moving to the Women of the Bible or New Testament rounds. This ensures every woman feels capable from the first question and settles into the group before the difficulty increases.

Round 1 of 4
👩 Women of the Bible (15 Questions)
Ruth • Esther • Mary • Deborah • Lydia • Rahab • Priscilla
1 Which woman said, "Whither thou goest, I will go; and where thou lodgest, I will lodge: thy people shall be my people, and thy God my God"? ▼
Ruth — speaking to her mother-in-law Naomi after Naomi urged her to return to Moab.
📖 Ruth 1:16 KJV
2 Queen Esther fasted for how many days before approaching the king uninvited? ▼
Three days — Esther called all the Jews in Shushan to fast neither eating nor drinking for three days before she went to the king.
📖 Esther 4:16 KJV
3 Who was the prophetess and judge of Israel who led the army alongside Barak? ▼
Deborah — the only female judge mentioned in the Bible, who sat under a palm tree and judged Israel.
📖 Judges 4:4–9 KJV
4 Which woman in Acts 16 was a seller of purple whom the Lord opened the heart of to receive Paul's message? ▼
Lydia — Paul's first convert in Europe, a businesswoman from the city of Thyatira.
📖 Acts 16:14 KJV
5 Rahab hid two Israelite spies on her rooftop under what material? ▼
Stalks of flax — Rahab had laid the flax on the roof to dry, and she concealed the men under it.
📖 Joshua 2:6 KJV
6 Mary and Martha were the sisters of which man Jesus raised from the dead? ▼
Lazarus — Jesus wept at his tomb before calling him out after four days.
📖 John 11:1–44 KJV
7 Which woman was the first person to see the risen Jesus at the empty tomb? ▼
Mary Magdalene — she came to the tomb early while it was still dark and encountered the risen Christ.
📖 John 20:1, 14–18 KJV
8 Priscilla and her husband Aquila pulled aside which gifted preacher to teach him the way of God more perfectly? ▼
Apollos — an eloquent man mighty in the Scriptures, who knew only the baptism of John.
📖 Acts 18:24–26 KJV
9 What did the angel Gabriel tell Mary that would cause her to conceive the Son of God? ▼
The Holy Ghost shall come upon thee, and the power of the Highest shall overshadow thee — Gabriel's announcement of the Incarnation.
📖 Luke 1:35 KJV
10 Hannah prayed desperately for a son and made a vow to the Lord. What did she promise to do if God gave her a child? ▼
Give the child to the Lord all the days of his life, and no razor shall come upon his head — the child born was Samuel.
📖 1 Samuel 1:11 KJV
11 Which Old Testament woman was known as a virtuous woman, and what book of the Bible describes her? ▼
The Proverbs 31 woman — described in Proverbs 31:10–31, her price is said to be far above rubies.
📖 Proverbs 31:10 KJV
12 Elizabeth was the mother of which prophet who prepared the way for Jesus? ▼
John the Baptist — Elisabeth was barren but conceived in her old age after the angel's announcement to Zacharias.
📖 Luke 1:13–17 KJV
13 Who was the mother of Moses, and what did she do to save his life when Pharaoh decreed all Hebrew boys be killed? ▼
Jochebed — she hid Moses for three months, then placed him in an ark of bulrushes on the river Nile.
📖 Exodus 2:2–3, 6:20 KJV
14 In the book of Acts, which woman was raised from the dead by Peter after she had died? She was known for making coats and garments for widows. ▼
Tabitha (also called Dorcas) — Peter knelt, prayed, and said, "Tabitha, arise."
📖 Acts 9:36–41 KJV
15 Naomi told her daughters-in-law to call her by what name after her husband and sons had died, meaning "bitter"? ▼
Mara — "Call me not Naomi, call me Mara: for the Almighty hath dealt very bitterly with me."
📖 Ruth 1:20 KJV

Round 2 of 4
📜 Old Testament Trivia (15 Questions)
Genesis • Moses • Psalms • Proverbs • The Prophets
16 On which day of creation did God create man? ▼
The sixth day — God also created the beasts of the earth on the sixth day, and saw that it was very good.
📖 Genesis 1:26–31 KJV
17 How many plagues did God send upon Egypt before Pharaoh let Israel go? ▼
Ten plagues — beginning with the water turned to blood and ending with the death of the firstborn.
📖 Exodus 7–12 KJV
18 What is the very first verse of the Bible? ▼
"In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth." — the foundational declaration of Scripture.
📖 Genesis 1:1 KJV
19 Which Psalm begins with the words, "The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want"? ▼
Psalm 23 — one of the most beloved passages in all of Scripture, written by David.
📖 Psalm 23:1 KJV
20 What does Proverbs 31:30 say is the woman who shall be praised? ▼
"A woman that feareth the LORD, she shall be praised." — Favour is deceitful, and beauty is vain, but a woman who fears the Lord is praised.
📖 Proverbs 31:30 KJV
21 Isaiah 40:31 says those who wait upon the LORD shall renew their strength and mount up with wings as what? ▼
Eagles — "they shall mount up with wings as eagles; they shall run, and not be weary; and they shall walk, and not faint."
📖 Isaiah 40:31 KJV
22 How many books of the Old Testament are there? ▼
39 books — from Genesis to Malachi.
📖 Canon of Scripture
23 Which prophet was swallowed by a great fish and spent three days inside it? ▼
Jonah — the LORD prepared a great fish to swallow Jonah after he fled from his call to go to Nineveh.
📖 Jonah 1:17 KJV
24 How many commandments did God give Moses on Mount Sinai? ▼
Ten commandments — written by the finger of God upon two tables of stone.
📖 Exodus 20, Deuteronomy 5 KJV
25 What was the name of Abraham's first son, born of Hagar? ▼
Ishmael — the angel of the LORD told Hagar to call his name Ishmael, because the LORD had heard her affliction.
📖 Genesis 16:11 KJV
26 Jeremiah 29:11 says God knows the thoughts He thinks toward us — thoughts of peace and not of evil — to give us what? ▼
"An expected end" — also translated in other versions as "a future and a hope." The KJV reads: "to give you an expected end."
📖 Jeremiah 29:11 KJV
27 In the Valley of Dry Bones, which prophet spoke life to the bones at God's command? ▼
Ezekiel — in Ezekiel 37, God commanded him to prophesy to the dry bones and they came together, sinew by sinew.
📖 Ezekiel 37:1–14 KJV
28 David defeated Goliath using a sling and a stone. From which brook did he select five smooth stones? ▼
The brook (in the valley of Elah) — 1 Samuel 17:40 says he chose five smooth stones out of the brook and put them in a shepherd's bag.
📖 1 Samuel 17:40 KJV
29 Solomon asked God for wisdom rather than riches or long life. God granted his request — and also gave him what he did not ask for. What was that? ▼
Riches and honour — God said, "I have also given thee that which thou hast not asked, both riches, and honour."
📖 1 Kings 3:13 KJV
30 Proverbs 3:5 says, "Trust in the LORD with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own…" — complete the verse. ▼
"understanding" — "and lean not unto thine own understanding." The verse continues: "In all thy ways acknowledge him, and he shall direct thy paths."
📖 Proverbs 3:5–6 KJV

Round 3 of 4
✝️ New Testament Trivia (15 Questions)
The Gospels • The Church • Paul's Letters • The Life of Christ
31 John 3:16 begins, "For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son…" — complete the verse. ▼
"that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life."
📖 John 3:16 KJV
32 Jesus's first miracle was performed at a wedding in which city? ▼
Cana of Galilee — Jesus turned water into wine at a wedding feast where the wine had run out.
📖 John 2:1–11 KJV
33 How many loaves and fishes did Jesus use to feed five thousand people? ▼
Five loaves and two fishes — and there remained twelve baskets of fragments after the miracle.
📖 Matthew 14:17–20 KJV
34 Philippians 4:13 says, "I can do all things through…" — who or what gives the strength? ▼
Christ — "I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me."
📖 Philippians 4:13 KJV
35 On the Day of Pentecost, what appeared over the disciples that represented the presence of the Holy Spirit? ▼
Cloven tongues like as of fire — and they were all filled with the Holy Ghost and began to speak in other tongues.
📖 Acts 2:3–4 KJV
36 In the Sermon on the Mount, Jesus said, "Blessed are the pure in heart: for they shall…" — complete it. ▼
"see God." — one of the Beatitudes from the Sermon on the Mount.
📖 Matthew 5:8 KJV
37 Romans 8:28 says all things work together for good to them that love God. What is the second condition given? ▼
"to them who are the called according to his purpose." — both conditions are loving God and being called according to His purpose.
📖 Romans 8:28 KJV
38 Which disciple doubted the resurrection until he saw and touched Jesus's wounds? ▼
Thomas — he said he would not believe unless he put his finger into the print of the nails and his hand into Jesus's side.
📖 John 20:24–28 KJV
39 The fruit of the Spirit listed in Galatians 5:22 begins with three qualities — what are the first three? ▼
Love, joy, peace — followed by longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, and temperance.
📖 Galatians 5:22–23 KJV
40 Who wrote the most books of the New Testament? ▼
The Apostle Paul — he wrote 13 or 14 epistles, from Romans through Philemon (and likely Hebrews).
📖 Romans–Philemon KJV
41 2 Timothy 1:7 says God has not given us the spirit of fear, but of power and love — and what third thing? ▼
A sound mind — "For God hath not given us the spirit of fear; but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind."
📖 2 Timothy 1:7 KJV
42 In which city was Jesus born? ▼
Bethlehem — in Bethlehem of Judaea, as prophesied in Micah 5:2.
📖 Matthew 2:1 KJV
43 Who baptised Jesus in the river Jordan? ▼
John the Baptist — and when Jesus came up from the water, the Holy Spirit descended like a dove and a voice from heaven said, "This is my beloved Son."
📖 Matthew 3:13–17 KJV
44 According to 1 Corinthians 13:13, which three things abide — and which is the greatest? ▼
Faith, hope, and charity (love) — "and the greatest of these is charity."
📖 1 Corinthians 13:13 KJV
45 What was the name of the book Jesus opened in the synagogue in Nazareth and read from? ▼
The book of the prophet Isaiah — he read: "The Spirit of the Lord is upon me, because he hath anointed me to preach the gospel to the poor."
📖 Luke 4:17–18 KJV

Round 4 of 4 — Start Here for Mixed Groups!
😄 Fun Icebreaker Questions (15 Questions)
Accessible for all knowledge levels • Perfect for new believers and guests
46 How many days and nights did it rain during Noah's flood? ▼
40 days and 40 nights — "And rain was upon the earth forty days and forty nights."
📖 Genesis 7:12 KJV
47 What body of water did Moses part so the Israelites could cross on dry ground? ▼
The Red Sea — Moses stretched his rod over the sea and the LORD caused the sea to go back by a strong east wind.
📖 Exodus 14:21 KJV
48 Which fruit did Adam and Eve eat in the Garden of Eden that they were forbidden to eat? ▼
The fruit of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil — the Bible does not specify it was an apple. It says "fruit."
📖 Genesis 2:17; 3:6 KJV
49 What is the shortest verse in the Bible, consisting of just two words? ▼
"Jesus wept." — spoken at the tomb of Lazarus, showing the humanity and compassion of Christ.
📖 John 11:35 KJV
50 Daniel was thrown into the lions' den for praying to God. How many times a day did he pray? ▼
Three times a day — Daniel knelt upon his knees three times a day, praying and giving thanks before his God.
📖 Daniel 6:10 KJV
51 How many disciples did Jesus choose? ▼
Twelve disciples — also called apostles, chosen from among his followers to be with him and to preach.
📖 Matthew 10:1–4 KJV
52 What did the wise men follow to find baby Jesus? ▼
A star — "we have seen his star in the east, and are come to worship him."
📖 Matthew 2:2–9 KJV
53 Which book of the Bible has the most chapters? ▼
Psalms — with 150 chapters, it is the longest book in the Bible by chapter count.
📖 Book of Psalms KJV
54 Who built the ark that saved his family and animals from the great flood? ▼
Noah — God commanded Noah to build an ark of gopher wood with specific dimensions.
📖 Genesis 6:14 KJV
55 What was the name of the garden where Jesus prayed before his arrest? ▼
Gethsemane — Jesus said, "Sit ye here, while I go and pray yonder," then went a little further and fell on his face.
📖 Matthew 26:36 KJV
56 How many books are in the entire Bible? ▼
66 books — 39 in the Old Testament and 27 in the New Testament.
📖 Canon of Scripture
57 Jesus said, "I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by…" — complete it. ▼
"me." — "Jesus saith unto him, I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me."
📖 John 14:6 KJV
58 Samson's great strength was tied to his hair. Who cut it while he slept? ▼
Delilah — she called for a man to shave off the seven locks of his head while he slept, and his strength departed from him.
📖 Judges 16:19 KJV
59 Which angel appeared to Mary to announce that she would bear the Son of God? ▼
Gabriel — "And the angel came in unto her, and said, Hail, thou that art highly favoured, the Lord is with thee."
📖 Luke 1:26–28 KJV
60 What did Jesus say are the two greatest commandments, according to Matthew 22? ▼
Love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, soul, and mind — and love thy neighbour as thyself. "On these two commandments hang all the law and the prophets."
📖 Matthew 22:37–40 KJV

Tips for Making Bible Trivia Night Unforgettable

🎯
Balance the Teams
Mix newer believers with long-time Bible students on every team. This keeps competition fair and creates natural mentoring moments when someone explains an answer.
💬
Add One Discussion Prompt Per Round
After each round, ask one open question connected to the theme. "Which woman in Round 1 do you most relate to, and why?" turns trivia into testimony.
🏆
Celebrate Everyone, Not Just the Winner
Give a prize to the team that was the most gracious, the most encouraging, or the quickest to look up a verse when they weren't sure. It shifts the room from competition to community.
📝
Keep a Score Sheet Visible
Post the running score somewhere the whole room can see it. The suspense between rounds keeps energy high, especially when teams are neck and neck going into Round 4.
⏱
Timing Matters
Each round of 15 questions takes roughly 15–20 minutes. A full evening of all four rounds runs about 75–90 minutes — perfect for a ladies' night that has a clear, satisfying end time.
📖
Keep Bibles on the Table
Even if it's not a "look-it-up" game, having Bibles present shifts the atmosphere. Women naturally flip to a reference after hearing the answer — and the Word does the rest.
