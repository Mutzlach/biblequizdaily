📖 Why Testing Bible Knowledge Matters

Throughout the Gospels, Jesus asked His disciples questions to test their understanding and deepen their faith. In Matthew 16:13–15 (KJV), He asked: "Whom do men say that I the Son of man am?" — and then the deeper question: "But whom say ye that I am?"

Peter answered correctly — "Thou art the Christ, the Son of the living God" — and Jesus praised him for it. Testing knowledge isn't just an academic exercise. It reveals what you truly believe, and shows you what you still need to learn.

"Using Bible quiz questions in our Sunday School class completely transformed engagement! Students who were bored before now compete to answer questions correctly. One student told me, 'I never realized how much I didn't know about Moses until I took the quiz. Now I've read Exodus three times!' Bible quizzes aren't just fun — they inspire deeper study."

— Sarah M., Sunday School Teacher

The same principle applies whether you're preparing a Sunday School lesson, running a family devotion, or simply testing your own knowledge. Questions create engagement. Engagement leads to study. Study leads to transformation.

🎯 5 Creative Ways to Run These Questions

  1. Sunday School Competition: Divide into teams and keep score on the board. The winning team chooses the snack or gets first pick of seats next week!
  2. Bible Study Icebreaker: Start every session with 5 questions. It settles the room, focuses minds, and builds a habit of Scripture engagement between meetings.
  3. Family Devotion Game: Take turns asking questions at the dinner table. Make it silly — wrong answers get a funny consequence. Right answers earn a point toward a family reward.
  4. Youth Group Challenge: Run individual rounds across multiple weeks and keep a running leaderboard. Students will study just to move up the rankings.
  5. Self-Study Flashcards: Cut apart the questions and answers, separate them, and test yourself. Review the ones you missed until you've mastered every single one.
