What the Bible Really Says About Strength

True strength in Scripture does not come from human effort — it comes from God.

His power is made perfect in our weakness, not our performance.

The 21 verses below are drawn from across both Testaments, each with a short commentary explaining what it means in its original context and how it applies today. Every verse is from the World English Bible (Public Domain).

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Bible say about strength? ▼
Philippians 4:13 is most cited: I can do all things through Christ, who strengthens me. Isaiah 40:31 is equally beloved: Those who wait for Yahweh will renew their strength. Both ground strength in God rather than human willpower.
Where does strength come from according to the Bible? ▼
Nehemiah 8:10 gives the famous statement: the joy of Yahweh is your strength. Psalm 46:1 says God is our refuge and strength. Biblical strength always has its source in God.
What Bible verse to read when feeling weak? ▼
Isaiah 40:29-31 speaks directly to weakness: He gives power to the weak. He increases the strength of him who has no might. Even the youths shall faint and be weary. But those who wait for Yahweh will renew their strength.
